One Batsman Carried 34.6% of His Entire Team's Runs

Using SUM() OVER(PARTITION BY Team), I calculated the exact proportion of franchise runs scored by individual players.

  • SRH: Relies dangerously on David Warner (34.67% of all runs).
  • RCB: Heavily top-heavy: Kohli & ABD combine for a massive 50.01%.
  • Delhi Capitals: The gold standard of balance: Dhawan (27%), Pant (25%), and Iyer (24%) split the load perfectly.
SELECT Player, Team, Runs,
  ROUND(Runs * 100.0 /
  SUM(Runs) OVER(PARTITION BY Team), 2) AS Run_Share
FROM batsmen_2019;

05/10

The Most Improved Player Wasn't Who You'd Expect

Year-on-Year Trajectory (Strike Rate Velocity)

Spotting high-momentum assets using Year-on-Year (YoY) JOIN analysis. By joining the 2018 and 2019 datasets, I mapped changes in batting averages and strike rates.

While absolute statistics favor veteran stars, the acceleration vector belongs to Hardik Pandya:

  • Hardik Pandya: Batting average grew by +15.78, and Strike Rate surged by a massive +58.10 YoY.
  • Andre Russell: Average grew by +27.94, and Strike Rate jumped by +20.02.
SELECT a.Player,
  (b.Avg - a.Avg) AS Avg_Delta,
  (b.S_R - a.S_R) AS SR_Delta
FROM batsmen_2018 a
JOIN batsmen_2019 b ON a.Player = b.Player;
06/10

A 204 Strike Rate. And His Average Was 56.

Weighted Consistency Index

Score = (Avg × 0.5) + (SR × 0.3) + ((50s + (100s × 2)) × 5)

To separate players who had one lucky innings from genuinely consistent game-changers, I formulated a custom, weighted metric.

  • David Warner (127.76): High-volume, reliable runs.
  • Andre Russell (109.78): Historic, unmatched boundary rate.
  • KL Rahul (107.57): Consistent structural anchor.

Why this math? It rewards hundreds heavily (game-winners), while perfectly balancing sheer volume (Average) with scoring pace (Strike Rate).

The Bowler with the Best Economy Also Took the Most Wickets

The Magic Quadrant: High Wickets, Low Economy

A bowler who takes 3 wickets but leaks 12 runs an over hurts your team. I calculated a custom Bowling Impact Score to reward raw wicket volume while penalizing high economy rates.

Impact = (Wickets × 10) - (ER × 5) - (Avg × 0.5)

  • Rashid Khan: 21 Wickets, 6.74 Economy ➡️ 165.40 Score
  • Jasprit Bumrah: 17 Wickets, 6.89 Economy ➡️ 124.61 Score

By plotting Economy on an inverted X-axis against Wickets, the elite performers isolate themselves completely in the top-right "Magic Quadrant."
